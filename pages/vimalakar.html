Professional Journey

I was raised in a lower-middle-class family with an agricultural background in a small village of Vadyal, Adilabad district, Telangana, India. As the first in my family to receive an education, I completed my schooling at a Government school in Vadyal. My keen interest in the medical field led to a high score in EAMCET, allowing me to graduate from the esteemed Gandhi Medical College, Hyderabad, India.

My passion for surgery and dedication to the medical profession led me to pursue a DNB in General Surgery at Kamineni Hospital, where I developed a special interest in the Hepatobiliary system. My dream of specializing in surgical gastroenterology came true when I joined MMHRC, Madurai for my DNB in Surgical Gastroenterology, where I was nurtured and trained to become the surgeon I am today.

As a young and enthusiastic surgeon, I joined the Department of Surgical Gastroenterology at Sunshine Hospital, Secunderabad, where I played a key role in establishing a successful liver transplant program. I also focused on training my team in minimally invasive bariatric surgeries. I have always been driven by a deep sense of responsibility to serve the underprivileged, and this is reflected in my efforts to educate my patients and use them as tools to spread awareness within their communities through peer group meetings and social media outreach.

My passion for teaching budding surgeons has led me to organize numerous workshops on intricate surgical procedures. I have published many articles and delivered lectures at national and international forums, always striving to present complex concepts in a simple and understandable way. As the chief reviewer for both national and international journals, I am also a primary investigator for several ongoing studies. Additionally, I have authored chapters in surgical gastroenterology textbooks.

In addition to my medical career, I have a love for poetry and have composed many works in my mother tongue, Telugu. I later released a poetry book titled "Kavi Rayani Kavithalu". My contributions to medicine have been recognized with the prestigious “Vaidya Shree” and “Best Doctor” awards in 2020, and in 2021, I was honored with the title of Fellow of the American College of Surgeons (FACS).

I am also the founder of Kunjara Yatra, an initiative aimed at raising awareness about the early detection of GI cancers, the importance of diet, and maintaining a healthy BMI. As part of this program, we conduct a comprehensive range of screening tests, including ultrasound, endoscopy, proctoscopy, Liver Function Tests (LFT), lipid profile, complete blood profile (CBP), serum creatinine, random blood sugar (RBS), ECG, and 2D-echo. These tests help in the early identification of GI cancers and other related conditions, enabling timely intervention and treatment.

Through this initiative, we also educate communities on how lifestyle factors, such as diet and weight management, significantly impact the risk of developing GI cancers. Our goal is to empower individuals to make informed decisions about their health, thereby reducing the prevalence and burden of GI cancers, particularly in underserved areas.
